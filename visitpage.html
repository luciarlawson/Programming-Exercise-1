5 essential experiences for North Berwick

1. Scottish Seabird Centre

The Scottish Seabird Centre in North Berwick is a popular wildlife attraction focused on marine conservation and seabird life. Located by the harbor, it offers interactive exhibits, live-streamed footage from remote cameras on Bass Rock, the Isle of May, and other local seabird colonies. Visitors can observe thousands of gannets, puffins, and other seabirds up close without disturbing their habitats.The centre has a small cafe with amazing views over the Firth of Forth and all the local islands.

The center also hosts boat tours, educational programs, and conservation initiatives to protect Scotland’s coastal wildlife. With a café and stunning seaside views, it's a great spot for families, nature lovers, and birdwatchers.

2. Bass Rock Boat Trip

The Scottish Seabird Centre in North Berwick offers exciting Bass Rock boat tours, giving visitors a close-up view of the world’s largest Northern single island gannet colony. These tours take you around the dramatic volcanic island, where thousands of seabirds nest on its rugged cliffs.

Depending on the tour type, you may also see puffins, seals, and even dolphins. Options range from high-speed RIB boats for adventurous travelers to leisurely catamaran cruises for a more relaxed experience. These trips provide an incredible wildlife spectacle and stunning coastal views, making them a must-do for nature lovers and photographers.

3. North Berwick Beaches

Relax on the sandy shores, go for a coastal walk, or enjoy watersports. North Berwick East Beach has a shallow paddling pool that fills up at high tide. This is the perfect beach for families with young kids. West Beach is better for older kids and/or adults that would like to spend the day kayaking or paddleboarding.

4. North Berwick Law

Walking up North Berwick Law is a rewarding hike that offers panoramic views over North Berwick, the Firth of Forth, and beyond. This 613-foot (187m) volcanic hill is a prominent landmark, topped with a replica whale jawbone, a nod to the town’s maritime heritage. From the car park at the bottom, The Law takes can be walked up and back down in just oven an hour. Depending on how long you spen looking at the views!

5. Golfing in North Berwick

North Berwick is a premier golfing destination, home to some of Scotland’s finest links courses. The North Berwick Golf Club (West Links), established in 1832, is one of the world’s oldest clubs. Nearby, The Glen Golf Club (East Links) offers a more relaxed yet equally scenic experience, with clifftop holes overlooking Bass Rock. The area is part of Scotland’s Golf Coast, known for its natural landscapes, historic courses, and year-round playability. With a mix of challenge, history, and breathtaking coastal views, North Berwick is a must-visit for any golf enthusiast.
