About

North Berwick is a small seaside town on Scotland’s east coast, just a 30 minute train ride from Edinburgh. Being a seaside town, it has a beautiful coastline with sandy beaches and a local harbour. To see all of north berwick and the surrounding areas visitors can take a walk up The Law.

One of North Berwick’s top attractions is the Scottish Seabird Centre, where visitors can learn about local marine life and observe puffins, gannets, and other seabirds through interactive exhibits and live camera feeds from nearby islands. Leaving from the harbour, a boat trip to Bass Rock, home to one of the world’s largest gannet colonies, is a must.

For breathtaking views, visitors can walk up North Berwick Law, an extinct volcanic hill that offers amazing views over the town and coastline. History enthusiasts can explore Tantallon Castle, a dramatic cliffside fortress with a fascinating medieval past, or discover the ruins of Dirleton Castle nearby.

East Loathian has around 20 golf courses with a couple located in North Berwick itself. North Berwick Golf Club and The Glen Golf Club, both offering challenging play with spectacular sea views.

The town itself is filled with charming streets lined with independent shops, cozy cafés, and seafood restaurants, perfect for a mooch. Whether you're strolling along the beach, exploring the town’s history, or indulging in fresh seafood, North Berwick is a delightful coastal retreat with something for everyone.
